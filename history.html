Islamic History

The rich heritage of Islamic civilization

Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) - 570-632 CE

The final prophet who received the Quran and established the first Islamic state in Medina.

The Rightly Guided Caliphs (632-661 CE)

  • Abu Bakr (632-634) - First caliph, compiled the Quran
  • Umar ibn al-Khattab (634-644) - Expanded Islamic empire, established Islamic calendar
  • Uthman ibn Affan (644-656) - Standardized Quran text
  • Ali ibn Abi Talib (656-661) - Last rightly guided caliph

The Umayyad Caliphate (661-750 CE)

Expanded Islam to Spain, North Africa, and Central Asia. Built the Dome of the Rock in Jerusalem.

The Abbasid Caliphate (750-1258 CE)

Golden Age of Islam - advancements in science, mathematics, medicine, and philosophy. Established House of Wisdom in Baghdad.

Great Islamic Scholars

  • Imam Abu Hanifa (699-767) - Founder of Hanafi school
  • Imam Malik ibn Anas (711-795) - Founder of Maliki school
  • Imam Al-Shafi'i (767-820) - Founder of Shafi'i school
  • Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal (780-855) - Founder of Hanbali school
  • Al-Ghazali (1058-1111) - Reviver of Islamic sciences
  • Ibn Taymiyyah (1263-1328) - Great Hanbali scholar

The Ottoman Empire (1299-1922)

Longest-lasting Islamic caliphate, conquered Constantinople, reached Vienna.
